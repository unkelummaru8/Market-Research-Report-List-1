<p><p>The robotic lawnmower market features two primary blade systems: pivoting blades and fixed blades. Pivoting blades rotate around a central point, adapting to terrain variations for a more uniform cut and reduced wear on the lawn. In contrast, fixed blades maintain a consistent height and angle, offering reliable cutting but less adaptability to uneven surfaces. Both systems cater to different consumer preferences and lawn care needs, impacting the overall performance and efficiency of robotic lawnmowers.</p></p>
