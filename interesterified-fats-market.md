<p><p>The interesterified fats market encompasses two main types: chemical and enzymatic interesterified fats. Chemical interesterified fats are produced through a chemical process that rearranges fatty acids on glycerol backbones, enhancing stability and functionality in food applications. Enzymatic interesterified fats, on the other hand, utilize specific enzymes to facilitate the rearrangement of fatty acids, offering more controlled and tailored properties. Both types are increasingly used in margarine, baked goods, and other food products to improve texture, melting behavior, and nutritional profiles.</p></p>
